# Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist for Students

1. **Never share your UPI PIN to receive money:** Remember that a UPI PIN is *only* required when you are sending money, never when receiving a payment.
2. **Verify unexpected payment requests:** If someone claims to be from the college asking for urgent UPI payments, hang up and call the official university department directly to confirm.
3. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Turn on 2FA for your email, social media, and banking applications to add an extra layer of security.
4. **Beware of public Wi-Fi:** Avoid logging into bank accounts, making payments, or accessing sensitive student portals while connected to unsecured campus or cafe Wi-Fi networks.
5. **Check URLs carefully:** Before entering login credentials, ensure the website address is correct and starts with "https" to avoid phishing clones.
6. **Update software regularly:** Keep your phone's operating system and all apps updated to patch known security vulnerabilities.
7. **Limit personal info on social media:** Do not publicly post your phone number, college ID cards, or real-time location, which scammers use to build targeted profiles.
8. **Report incidents immediately:** If you suspect you have been defrauded or harassed, immediately freeze your bank accounts and report the crime to the **National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)** or call the **Helpline 1930**.
