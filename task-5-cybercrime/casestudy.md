# Case Study: The "Urgent Fee" UPI Scam Targeting College Students

**What the crime is:** UPI fraud is a form of financial cybercrime where scammers deceive victims into authorizing direct, unrecoverable money transfers from their bank accounts using the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) system.

**How it happens (step-by-step):**
1. The scammer gathers basic information about the student from social media or leaked databases.
2. The scammer poses as a university official, hostel warden, or courier service and calls the student, claiming there is an "urgent, unpaid fee" or a package that cannot be delivered.
3. The scammer creates a sense of extreme panic, threatening that the student's registration will be cancelled or the package destroyed if not paid within minutes.
4. The scammer sends a UPI payment request directly to the student's phone.
5. In a rush, the panicked student enters their secure UPI PIN, believing they are paying a legitimate fee, but instead authorizing a direct transfer to the scammer.

**Who is usually targeted:** College students, especially first-years who are unfamiliar with official administrative processes, are living away from home for the first time, and are highly anxious about rule compliance.

**Consequences:** The immediate consequence is absolute financial loss, as UPI transfers are instantaneous and very difficult to reverse. For a college student living on a tight budget, losing ₹5,000 to ₹10,000 can cause severe financial distress. Furthermore, the victim's phone number may be added to a "sucker list" and targeted for future phishing attacks, compounding the long-term risk.
